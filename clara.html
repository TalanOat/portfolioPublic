Summary

This project, grounded in over six months of mental health research, supports individuals facing mental health challenges while helping general users better understand their emotions. It provides an accessible, low-cost alternative to complex, expensive apps with weak privacy protections. User data remains securely on the device, with minimal and optional use of third-party services. By combining language models with visualizations, it identifies meaningful patterns such as emotional triggers and highlights the connection between environment and well-being through mapping features. Its robust design allows for seamless integration of additional features in future updates.

This app is designed to work alongside exisiting therapy through providing a historic log of emotional patterns and triggers. It is not a replacement for therapy or medication.

What I Took Away

Technology as a Positive

Recent headlines underscore the growing need for accessible mental health support while also revealing the downsides of technology use. This highlights the importance of developers demonstrating the positive impact technology.

Design Simplicity

Limited screen size and resources drove me to create an accessible interface optimised for battery life and user experience. Every button was purposefully included, with each iteration refining and streamlining the design. By leveraging native components, I ensured the app was intuitive for users already familiar with their respective platforms.

Robust Planning

This was one of my most extensive projects, requiring me to dive into unfamiliar technology and quickly learn its intricacies. It provided valuable experience in planning, time management, and adapting to new challenges. I successfully achieved over 95% of the project goals on time and at a quality level that met my high standards.

What Could be added

Text Journal Scanning

Some users may prefer scanning handwritten journals to integrate them with app features like pattern recognition and mapping. Physical journaling has unique benefits, but analyzing patterns can be difficult. By providing a digital backup, the app ensures entries are accessible anytime, and users can enhance privacy by discarding the physical journal after scanning.

Social Connectivity

The app could include a social connectivity feature, enabling users to connect anonymously and share journal prompts, benefiting the entire community.

More Analysis Models

Enhanced analysis models could better support users on their mental health journey. These models could evolve with user feedback to address specific needs, such as detecting patterns of self-criticism or predicting periods of low mood by linking words to historical data. For example, if low mood follows mentions of family, the app might suggest a hidden personal issue for users to explore in therapy, offering valuable insights.
